1. **Investigations overview**. This page shows all past investigations, sorted by date. Click into an investigation for more detail. 

   <Frame>
     ![Investigations](/images/investigations.png)
   </Frame>
2. **Investigation summary**. Click into an investigation for a breakdown of what happened. 

   <Frame>
     ![Investigation](/images/investigation.png)
   </Frame>
3. **Reports**. On the right hand side of an investigation page, you’ll find a minimum of 3 investigation reports. 
   1. [<u>problem.md</u>](http://problem.md) - This report includes a description of the problem as understood and preliminary investigation.
   2. theory/hypothesis\_[x.md](http://x.md) - This report shows the root cause hypothesis that was tested following dependency investigation. Depending on how many root cause hypotheses were tested, you may see multiple reports. 
   3. [<u>report.md</u>](http://report.md) - This report shows you a summary of the investigation.

   <Frame>
     ![Reports](/images/reports.png)
   </Frame>
4. **Summary**. On the left hand side of an investigation page, you’ll see the full summary of the investigation including:
   1. Alert - The original alert

   <Frame>
     ![Screenshot2026 02 24at14 27 02](/images/Screenshot2026-02-24at14.27.02.png)
   </Frame>
5. Description - A description of the issue in plain English.

   <Frame>
     ![Screenshot2026 02 24at14 27 47](/images/Screenshot2026-02-24at14.27.47.png)
   </Frame>
6. Process - The process OpenSRE followed to investigate the issue including:
   1. Runbooks and artifacts consulted by the AI agent

      <Frame>
        ![Screenshot2026 02 24at14 28 25](/images/Screenshot2026-02-24at14.28.25.png)
      </Frame>
   2. Plan - the steps the AI agent(s) took to investigate the issue

      <Frame>
        ![Screenshot2026 02 24at14 29 01](/images/Screenshot2026-02-24at14.29.01.png)
      </Frame>
   3. Investigation summary and recommended next steps

      <Frame>
        ![Screenshot2026 02 24at14 29 35](/images/Screenshot2026-02-24at14.29.35.png)
      </Frame>
